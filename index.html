Abstract

Long-tail events pose a significant challenge in testing and evaluating autonomous driving planners, as they necessitate a substantial increase in the volume of real data to be collected. Several approaches have been presented for the automatic generation of traffic scenarios that could contain such long-tail events. Unfortunately, data-driven generative models for synthetic scenarios require extensive training data and often lack fine-grained control over the output. A practical solution involves the manual creation of specific critical scenarios by domain experts; however, this does not scale well.

To address such scalability issue, we propose an agentic framework for natural language-guided scenario augmentation. This approach leverages language as a more natural user interface and an abstraction layer to convey intent, which allows for the sampling of detailed realizations based on user intent. In our extensive evaluation, we ablate different prompting strategies and demonstrate the ability of our framework to follow user intent, generate scenarios which look convincing to human domain experts and pose a challenge to state-of-the-art planning algorithms.
